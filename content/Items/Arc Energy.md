A mysterious energy generated within the perpetual [[Arc Storm]]. Its basic effects are similar to that of conventional lightning, emitting a purplish-white light when travelling through air and when stored and channeled in [[#Aetherium]].

By manipulating the geometric arrangement of the energy, the energy can be made to affect its environment in strange ways. This is usually done by carving Aetherium cores into carefully designed patters. At its most basic, this resembles a runic system, but its rapid development has reached the point where the complexity of the patterns approaches that of modern day electronic processors and controllers are generally referred to as circuits.

It is used as a source of energy for the many devices that are developed and produced within [[Hyperius]]. 

# Aetherium
Formed exclusively within [[Arc Tree]]s' cores, Aetherium is the only known way to store and and control the effects of Arc Energy. Generally, devices harnessing the power of Arc Energy split the functions of energy storage and effect creation, due to their different constraints.

# Storage
The maximum amount of Arc Energy that can be stored by an individual crystal of Aetherium scales to the third power of the crystal's volume (i.e. to the ninth power of diameter). Upon reaching and exceeding a crystal's maximum capacity it will slowly leak its energy into the surrounding area (yes, this is a health concern). The rate at which energy leaks is proportional to how far above its capacity a core is charged. By charging a core far past its limit very quickly (through Arc transfer mechanisms), it will collapse and explode.

# Usage
There are currently two main ways in which complex behavior is created from Arc Energy. Generally, the complexity of a circuit scales with the intricacy and surface area of the circuit.
## Carving
Arc Cores can be modified directly or sliced into standardized shapes before having their effects carved into them. This form of circuitry allows for greater energy outputs and therefore more powerful effects. 
Large cores are almost exclusively carved as this method makes best use of them by combing the greater energy output with the larger area for more complex circuits.
## Molding
Aetherium cores can also be ground into powder and molded along with some other moldable material as a medium. This allows for higher automation of the process and does not depend on the size of the cores being used. Therefore, this form of circuitry is much cheaper to produce and is what is used in the majority of low-grade devices.
The powdered form of the Aetherium inhibits its ability to conduct energy and so energy output from devices created using molding is limited compared to carved circuits.
